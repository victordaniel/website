Research Statement

My research is driven by a commitment to fundamental inquiry in deep learning — establishing not just what neural networks can do, but why they work, through a combination of formal theorems, supporting lemmas, and controlled empirical experiments. The clearest expression of this is my work on the GONO (Gradient Directional Consistency) Framework, currently submitted to NeurIPS 2026 — one of the premier venues in machine learning. GONO interrogates whether the direction of gradient updates carries exploitable signal for improving neural network training — establishing this through formal theoretical analysis backed by systematic experimentation. This solo-authored work reflects the depth and independence of my research thinking.

My applied research has produced a coherent body of work in Graph Neural Networks for anomaly detection — including a GNN-based encoder for node and edge anomaly detection (Electronics, MDPI, 2023 — 11 citations), a multi-GNN attention fusion framework for fake news detection (International Journal of Cognitive Computing in Engineering, Elsevier, 2025), and a federated semi-supervised GNN for privacy-preserving Gestational Diabetes prediction (2026). My emerging frontier is fundamental LLM research — applying the same theorem-and-experiment methodology to open questions in LLM theory. I maintain 104 public GitHub repositories as a direct expression of my commitment to open, reproducible science.

Research Area 01

Graph Neural Networks & Anomaly Detection

Designing GNN encoders and multi-attention fusion frameworks for node, edge, and subgraph-level anomaly detection in attributed and social networks. Published in Electronics (MDPI, SCIE) and International Journal of Cognitive Computing in Engineering (Elsevier, 2025). Work spans from graph clustering and GCN exploration through to multi-attention subgraph detection — a coherent, deepening research focus over multiple years.

11 Citations on the MDPI SCIE paper alone
Research Area 02

Deep Learning Optimisation Theory

Formal theoretical investigation of gradient directional consistency as a complementary optimisation signal — the GONO Framework. Solo-authored. Submitted to NeurIPS 2026, the premier machine learning conference worldwide. Available on arXiv:2605.06575. The paper establishes the framework through formal theoretical analysis backed by systematic experimentation, contributing an original optimisation signal as a complement to conventional loss-based descent.

NeurIPS 2026 — premiere ML conference submission
Research Area 03

Federated Learning & LLM Fundamentals

Privacy-preserving semi-supervised GNNs with prototype-guided pseudo-labelling applied to Gestational Diabetes Mellitus prediction. Simultaneously, applying the same theorem-and-experiment methodology to open questions in Large Language Model theory — the mathematical basis of in-context learning, the geometry of attention mechanisms, and the conditions under which scale produces generalisation. A new frontier with the same rigorous approach.

104 Open GitHub repositories — fully reproducible science
